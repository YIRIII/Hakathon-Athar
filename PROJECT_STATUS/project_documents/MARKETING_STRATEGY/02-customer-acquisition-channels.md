## Per-Channel Deep Dive

### QR Codes at Heritage Sites (Score: 4.55 — #1)

**Overview**: Athar's unique distribution advantage. Physical QR codes at 10-12 heritage sites in Makkah/Madinah directly reach visitors at the moment of need. Scan → instant PWA → immediate value. No app download, no search, no discovery friction.

**Estimated CAC**: ~$0 per user (cost is QR printing + Heritage Commission permission, not per-user)

**Pros**:
- Reaches pilgrims at the exact point of need (standing at a heritage site)
- Zero download friction (PWA, not app store)
- Validated model: Bebot (Japan) proved QR → AI chatbot at tourism scale
- Saudi already deploys QR on street signs — behavior is normalized

**Cons**:
- Requires Heritage Commission approval for physical placement (Antiquities Law)
- Limited to visitors who physically visit the 10-12 sites
- Scale limited by number of sites with QR codes

**Bootstrap alternative**: GPS-triggered notifications when near heritage sites (no physical QR needed; no permission required)

### Digital Certificate Social Sharing (Score: 4.45 — #2)

**Overview**: Users earn heritage passport stamps and shareable certificates. Every share on Instagram/Twitter/Snapchat is free advertising to the sharer's network. Saudis spend 3h 6m daily on social media (Sprinklr, 2025) — certificates tap into this behavior.

**Estimated CAC**: ~$0 (built-in product mechanic)

**Pros**:
- Viral loop: visit site → earn stamp → share certificate → friends discover Athar
- Saudis' social media usage is 30% above global average
- Certificates are aspirational content (heritage education, not gaming)
- Each share reaches an average 500-1000 followers

**Cons**:
- Requires users to first visit a site and engage (chicken-and-egg with initial adoption)
- Sharing rate unknown — need to test certificate designs
- Network effect takes time to build

**Bootstrap alternative**: Same — it's a product feature, not a marketing spend.

### Social Media Organic (Score: 4.09 — #3)

**Overview**: Heritage site stories, historical facts, "did you know" posts, and user certificate reshares on Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat (top 3 platforms in Saudi Arabia). Content is inherently visual and shareable.

**Estimated CAC**: ~$0 (sweat equity; content comes from heritage research already done)

**Pros**:
- 35.33M Saudi social media users; 95.3% penetration
- Heritage content is visually rich — maps, historical images, site stories
- TikTok short-form: heritage "did you know" facts (30-60s) have viral potential
- Snapchat is #1 social platform in Saudi Arabia (22M+ users, DataReportal 2024)

**Cons**:
- Organic reach is declining on all platforms (algorithm changes)
- Requires consistent content creation (5-10 hrs/week founder time)
- Heritage content in Arabic needs cultural sensitivity review

**Bootstrap alternative**: 3-5 posts/week on Instagram + TikTok using heritage research already in RESEARCH.md. No tools needed beyond smartphone + Canva (free).

### Heritage Commission Partnership (Score: 4.00 — #4, post-PMF)

**Overview**: Government partnership unlocks physical QR placement, official endorsement, content co-creation, and potential cross-promotion through ministry channels (Ministry of Hajj & Umrah has millions of followers).

**Estimated CAC**: ~$0 per user if achieved (government distribution)

**Pros**:
- Instant credibility and trust with pilgrim audience
- Physical QR code placement rights (regulatory moat)
- Access to official heritage content and imagery
- Potential co-funding of platform development

**Cons**:
- Government partnerships take months to formalize
- May impose content/design requirements
- Risk of dependency on a single partner

**Bootstrap alternative**: Present hackathon prototype as proof-of-concept; request informal endorsement before formal partnership.
