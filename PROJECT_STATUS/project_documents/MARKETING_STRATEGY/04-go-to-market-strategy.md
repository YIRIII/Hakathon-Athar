## PLG vs Sales-Led Decision

### Decision Tree Analysis

| Factor | Value | Implication |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Expected ACV** | $0 (free) to $64/year (premium SAR 50/yr) | Firmly PLG territory — too low for sales |
| **Buyer persona** | Non-technical pilgrims/tourists (35-64 age, 190+ countries) | Must be self-serve; zero-touch onboarding via QR |
| **Product complexity** | Low (scan QR → explore) | Users get value instantly without guidance |
| **Self-serve potential** | Very High (QR scan = instant access to PWA) | No app download, no account required for basic use |
| **Competitive GTM** | Nusuk = government mandate; Visit Saudi = paid media; SirahMaps = academic WOM | No competitor uses PLG for heritage |
| **B2B component** | Heritage Commission ($2-5K/mo) | Small B2B overlay on top of PLG base |

**Recommended GTM Model:** **Product-Led Growth (PLG) with B2B overlay**

**Rationale**: Athar's ACV for consumers is $0-$64/year — far below the threshold for any sales motion. The QR-to-PWA model IS the growth engine: the product distributes itself through physical QR codes at heritage sites. Users get value in seconds (scan → heritage content). No human touch required. The B2B component (Heritage Commission dashboard) is an overlay that comes later, driven by PLG traction data ("X thousand visitors used Athar at your sites last month").

### Growth Loop

**Primary loop**: **Viral loop via shareable heritage certificates**

**How it works for Athar**: Visitor scans QR at heritage site → explores content → earns heritage stamp → completes collection → generates shareable certificate → posts on Instagram/TikTok/Snapchat → friends see certificate → friend visits Makkah/Madinah → scans QR at site → cycle repeats. The certificate is both the reward AND the distribution mechanism.

**Secondary loop**: **Physical distribution loop via QR codes**

QR codes at sites reach new visitors daily without any marketing spend. Every pilgrim who visits the physical site sees the QR code. With 37.5M combined visitors to Makkah/Madinah annually, this is a perpetual distribution channel.

## Positioning & Messaging

### Value Proposition

**For** Hajj/Umrah pilgrims and heritage-curious visitors **who** want to discover the rich history of Makkah and Madinah's heritage sites but have no guide, **Athar** is a heritage discovery platform **that** instantly transforms your visit with interactive maps, AI-powered heritage stories, and a digital passport. **Unlike** Nusuk (logistics only) or paid ziarat tours ($50-200), **we** provide free, on-site heritage education via QR codes — no download, no guide, no cost.

### Messaging by Persona

| Persona | Primary Pain Point | Key Message | Proof Point |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Ahmad (International Pilgrim)** | No affordable heritage guide; language barrier; limited time | "Scan. Discover. Learn. Heritage sites come alive in your language." | AI chatbot speaks 8+ languages; QR = instant access |
| **Sarah (Saudi Family)** | Kids find heritage boring; no gamified experience | "Turn heritage visits into an adventure your children will remember" | Digital passport stamps; knowledge badges; shareable certificates |
| **Dr. Ali (Heritage Commission)** | No digital engagement data; $53B invested but no measurement | "Finally measure what visitors do at your heritage sites" | Visitor analytics; QR scan data; engagement metrics |
