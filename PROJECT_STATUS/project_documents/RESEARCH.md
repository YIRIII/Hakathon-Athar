## 2. Competitor Analysis

### Direct Competitors (Saudi Arabia)

#### Nusuk (nusuk.sa) — Official Hajj & Umrah Platform
- **Developer**: Ministry of Hajj and Umrah
- **Users**: 40+ million registered from 190 countries; 30M downloads (150% YoY increase)
- **Features**: Umrah permit booking, Rawdah appointments, AI crowd density, transport reservations, hotel/flight bookings, offline capability
- **Pricing**: Free
- **Strengths**: Mandatory for Umrah 2026; massive user base; government-backed; 100+ digital services
- **Weaknesses**: Zero heritage discovery, no historical site exploration, no QR-based content, no AI chatbot guide, no gamification
- **Gap for Athar**: Nusuk handles pilgrimage logistics only — Athar fills the heritage discovery gap for 40M+ Nusuk users

Sources: https://www.spa.gov.sa/en/N2403190, https://www.eureporter.co/world/saudi-arabia/2025/11/07/nusuk-surpasses-40-million-users/

#### Webook (webook.com)
- **Users**: 7+ million across 160 countries; SAR 1B+ in ticket sales
- **Features**: Event ticketing, flights/hotels, restaurants, live streaming
- **Pricing**: Commission-based
- **Weaknesses**: Entertainment/events platform, not heritage discovery
- **Gap for Athar**: Different value proposition entirely; potential partnership opportunity

Source: https://www.arabnews.com/node/2598526/business-economy

#### Visit Saudi App (visitsaudi.com)
- **Developer**: Saudi Tourism Authority
- **Users**: ~2,640 monthly iOS downloads
- **Features**: Tourism directory, basic interactive map, events calendar
- **Weaknesses**: General-purpose; heritage content is surface-level listings only; no on-site features
- **Gap for Athar**: Athar provides deep, immersive heritage discovery vs. Visit Saudi's broad directory

#### Historic Jeddah App
- **Developer**: Ministry of Culture
- **Features**: Interactive map of Al-Balad, route planning, historical content, multilingual
- **Weaknesses**: Limited to Jeddah's Al-Balad only; no QR, no AI, no certificates
- **Significance**: Closest Saudi app to Athar's concept — validates market demand

Source: https://spa.gov.sa/en/N2322298

#### Heritage Eye (heritage-eye.vercel.app)
- **Developer**: Ministry of Culture
- **Features**: AR virtual tours of heritage sites
- **Weaknesses**: Virtual/remote only, not on-site discovery

#### SirahMaps (sirahmaps.com)
- **Features**: Makkah interactive heritage map with voice narrations and 3D animations for 37+ features and 117+ landmarks
- **Significance**: Closest existing comparable to Athar
- **Differentiation**: Athar adds AI chatbot, gamification, QR-code-first PWA approach

Source: https://sirahmaps.com/

### International Competitors

#### izi.TRAVEL
- 25,000 audio tours, 2,500 cities, 137 countries, 50+ languages
- Supports QR in museums, offline downloads, user-created content
- **Gap**: Very limited Saudi content; no AI chatbot; no certificates

#### SmartGuide
- B2B digital audio guides, QR + Bluetooth beacons, visitor analytics
- EUR 0.30-1.00 per user per destination
- **Gap**: B2B tool, not consumer-facing

#### Bebot (Japan)
- AI chatbot for tourists via QR (no app needed), multilingual
- Deployed at Tokyo Station, Narita Airport, Tokyo Metro
- **Significance**: Validates QR + AI chatbot model for tourism
- **Gap**: Japan-only; logistics focused, not heritage education

#### Google Arts & Culture
- 2,000+ institution partners, 80+ countries, AI audio guides, 3D models
- **Gap**: Extremely broad; minimal Saudi/Islamic heritage; no on-site features

### Competitive Landscape Summary

| Competitor | Heritage Focus | Smart Maps | On-Site QR | AI Chatbot | Certificates | Makkah/Madinah |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nusuk | None | No | No | No | No | Logistics only |
| Visit Saudi | Surface-level | Basic | No | No | No | Listings only |
| Historic Jeddah | Deep (Jeddah) | Yes | No | No | No | No |

## 4. Technical Feasibility

### Tech Stack Validation

| Component | Recommendation | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Frontend | Next.js + React | Strong RTL/i18n support via next-intl; well-documented Arabic web dev |
| Maps | Leaflet + Mapbox tiles | Free open-source renderer + quality tiles; offline support; 50K free web loads/mo |
| QR Scanner | qr-scanner (Nimiq) | 2-3x higher detection rate than alternatives; zero misreads |
| Architecture | PWA (Progressive Web App) | Zero-friction QR-to-content; no app download; service worker caching |
| CMS | Strapi (hackathon: JSON files) | Open-source headless CMS; Arabic/RTL support; REST + GraphQL |
| Hosting | Vercel (hackathon) → AWS/Oracle SA (production) | Free tier for Next.js; Saudi data residency for production |

### AI Chatbot Stack

| Component | Hackathon | Production |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| LLM | Gemini 2.0 Flash-Lite ($0.075/$0.30 per 1M tokens) | Jais (Arabic-native) + GPT-4o-mini fallback |
| Speech-to-Text | Google Cloud STT (Gulf Arabic model) | Deepgram or DeepVA+Lisan (Saudi dialect) |
| Text-to-Speech | Google Cloud TTS (1M free chars/mo) | ElevenLabs (most natural Arabic) |
| Architecture | RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) | Vector DB + knowledge base → LLM |

### Cost Per AI Conversation

~7,500 tokens per visit (5 exchanges). At scale:
- Gemini Flash-Lite: ~$0.0008/visit → $8/day at 10K visits
- GPT-4o-mini: ~$0.003/visit → $30/day at 10K visits

### Connectivity at Heritage Sites

**Excellent coverage** — NOT a blocker:
- 5G coverage: 98% Makkah, 99% Madinah
- 13,776 cell towers deployed for Mecca/holy places
- 10,500+ Wi-Fi access points across Makkah, Madinah
- Free Wi-Fi at Grand Mosque, Prophet's Mosque, Mina, Arafat
- Mobile speeds: 264 Mbps Makkah, 336 Mbps Madinah

Sources:
- https://scenenow.com/Buzz/Free-Wi-Fi-WIll-Be-Rolled-Out-Across-All-Major-Hajj-Sites-in-2025
- https://www.stats.gov.sa/en/w/gastat-there-are-13-776-towers

### Approach Validation

**QR-first PWA vs. Native App**: PWA is the right choice. Only 2.47% of museum visitors download apps — QR-to-PWA eliminates this barrier entirely. Bebot (Japan) proved QR + AI chatbot works for tourism at scale.

**AI Chatbot vs. Pre-recorded Audio**: Use BOTH. Pre-recorded audio for offline (cacheable, reliable), AI chatbot as enhanced interactive layer when online.

**AR (Augmented Reality)**: Skip for hackathon — overkill. Add as Phase 2 feature.

**Offline Strategy**: Service workers + pre-cached site content + static Q&A fallback per site. Predictive caching on QR scan loads nearby site content.

## 5. Regulatory Considerations

### Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)

- **Effective**: September 2023; full enforcement September 2024
- **Authority**: SDAIA
- **Requirements**: Consent for data collection, Arabic privacy notice, data subject rights (access, correct, delete)
- **Data localization**: Cross-border transfers restricted; use Saudi-based hosting (Oracle Jeddah, AWS Bahrain/Saudi)
- **Penalties**: Up to 2 years imprisonment + SAR 3M fine for sensitive data disclosure

Sources:
- https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2024/02/saudi-arabias-new-personal-data-protection-law-in-force
- https://securiti.ai/saudi-arabia-personal-data-protection-law/

### Heritage Site Regulations

**CRITICAL**: Law of Antiquities, Museums and Urban Heritage (2014):
- Posting unauthorized advertisements/markings at heritage sites: up to 1 year imprisonment + SAR 100,000 fine
- Physical QR code installation requires Heritage Commission approval
- **Recommendation**: Partner with Heritage Commission OR use GPS/Bluetooth beacons instead of physical QR

Source: https://saudigazette.com.sa/article/605137

### Photography Restrictions

- Photography banned inside Masjid al-Haram and Masjid an-Nabawi
- Cannot rely on user-generated photos from within holy mosques
- Use licensed archival imagery

### Business Requirements

- Tourism license required from Ministry of Tourism (5-10 business days)
- Arabic must be primary language for consumer-facing apps
- 100% foreign ownership now allowed for service companies
- Hackathon IP ownership varies — verify specific hackathon terms

### Content Sensitivity

- Heritage content must be framed within accepted Islamic religious interpretation
- Must not encourage shrine veneration or practices contrary to official religious stance
- Ministry of Culture's Digital Archiving Guide provides framework
