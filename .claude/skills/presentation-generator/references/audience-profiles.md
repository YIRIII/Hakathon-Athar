# Audience Profiles — Presentation Generator

This file defines the five audience profiles used by `/presentation-generator` to adapt presentation tone, content emphasis, slide selection, and language.

---

## 1. Investor Pitch

**Target**: VCs, angel investors, funding committees, accelerator panels

**Tone**: Confident, data-driven, opportunity-focused. Every claim backed by market data. Convey urgency ("why now") and scale ("how big").

**Opening Hook**: Lead with the market size and growth rate. Frame the problem as a massive, underserved opportunity. Use a striking statistic or cost figure to anchor the narrative.

**Content Emphasis**:
- TAM/SAM/SOM with growth trajectories
- Revenue model and unit economics
- Competitive moat and differentiation
- Traction signals (if any)
- Clear investment ask and use of funds
- Return timeline and exit potential

**Slide Priority**: Market Analysis, Revenue Projections, Competitive Landscape, Business Model, Budget/Investment Ask — all required. Technical details are minimal.

**Language Patterns**:
- EN: "opportunity", "return", "market cap", "traction", "scalable", "defensible moat", "unit economics", "runway"
- AR: "فرصة استثمارية", "عائد على الاستثمار", "حجم السوق", "نمو مستدام", "ميزة تنافسية", "نموذج أعمال قابل للتوسع"

**Call to Action**: "We're raising [amount] to [milestone]. Join us in [vision]."
- AR: "نسعى لجمع [المبلغ] لتحقيق [الهدف]. انضم إلينا في [الرؤية]."

**Speaker Notes Tone**: Include anticipated VC questions and counter-arguments. Note unit economics assumptions. Flag risks proactively with mitigations ready.

## 2. Partner Proposal

**Target**: Potential technology partners, business alliance candidates, integration partners, co-development prospects

**Tone**: Collaborative, technical, mutually beneficial. Emphasize shared value creation and ecosystem growth. Speak as peers, not as a pitch.

**Opening Hook**: Lead with the shared market opportunity or customer overlap. Frame the partnership as a force multiplier for both sides.

**Content Emphasis**:
- Integration architecture and technical compatibility
- Mutual customer base and market synergy
- Joint value proposition
- Technical capabilities and API compatibility
- Partnership model (co-develop, license, integrate)
- Shared roadmap potential

**Slide Priority**: Solution Overview, How It Works, Technical Architecture, Partnership Opportunity, Traction/Milestones — all required. Revenue/budget slides are optional.

**Language Patterns**:
- EN: "integration", "synergy", "mutual value", "ecosystem", "co-development", "complementary capabilities", "joint go-to-market"
- AR: "تكامل", "تعاون مشترك", "قيمة متبادلة", "منظومة تقنية", "تطوير مشترك", "قدرات متكاملة", "شراكة استراتيجية"

**Call to Action**: "Let's explore a pilot integration. Here's what a 90-day proof of concept looks like."
- AR: "دعونا نستكشف تكاملاً تجريبياً. إليكم ما يبدو عليه إثبات المفهوم خلال 90 يوماً."

**Speaker Notes Tone**: Include technical integration details, API-level notes, SDK compatibility. Note where customization would be needed for the specific partner.

## 3. Customer Demo

**Target**: Prospective customers, end users, early adopters, beta testers

**Tone**: Benefit-focused, empathetic, clear. Speak in the user's language, not technical jargon. Show, don't tell. Make the problem feel personal and the solution feel effortless.

**Opening Hook**: Lead with the problem the user faces daily. Use emotional framing — the frustration, risk, or cost of the status quo. Then reveal the solution as relief.

**Content Emphasis**:
- Problem/pain point (make it relatable)
- Solution demonstration (visual walkthrough)
- Key features and user benefits
- Pricing and value proposition
- How to get started
- Social proof / use cases (if available)

**Slide Priority**: Problem, Solution Overview, How It Works, Key Features, Business Model/Pricing, Call to Action — all required. Skip technical, market, and investment slides.

**Language Patterns**:
- EN: "you", "your safety", "simple", "protection", "peace of mind", "effortless", "get started in minutes"
- AR: "أنت", "سلامتك", "بسيط", "حماية", "راحة البال", "سهل الاستخدام", "ابدأ في دقائق"

**Call to Action**: "Download today" / "Start your free trial" / "Join the early access list"
- AR: "حمّل التطبيق الآن" / "ابدأ تجربتك المجانية" / "انضم لقائمة الوصول المبكر"

**Speaker Notes Tone**: Include demo talking points, FAQ responses, common objections and rebuttals. Note which features to emphasize for different user segments.

## 4. Government / Regulatory

**Target**: Government officials, grant committees, regulators, public safety agencies, policy makers

**Tone**: Formal, evidence-based, public-good oriented. Every claim must be substantiated with citations. Emphasize societal benefit, regulatory compliance, and alignment with public policy objectives.

**Opening Hook**: Lead with public safety statistics or societal impact data. Frame the project as addressing a documented public need. Cite government reports or official statistics.

**Content Emphasis**:
- Social impact and lives affected
- Regulatory compliance and alignment with existing frameworks
- Evidence base and research backing
- Economic benefit (job creation, cost savings to public systems)
- Data privacy and security posture
- Implementation feasibility in public sector contexts
- Budget transparency and accountability

**Slide Priority**: Problem/Opportunity, Market Analysis, Solution Overview, Social Impact/Compliance, Risk Mitigation, Budget/Investment Ask, Traction/Milestones, References — all required. Skip partnership and pricing slides.

**Language Patterns**:
- EN: "public safety", "evidence-based", "compliance", "economic impact", "lives saved", "regulatory framework", "accountability", "public-private partnership"
- AR: "السلامة العامة", "مبني على الأدلة", "الامتثال التنظيمي", "الأثر الاقتصادي", "إنقاذ الأرواح", "الإطار التنظيمي", "المساءلة", "شراكة بين القطاعين العام والخاص"

**Call to Action**: "We seek your support to bring this initiative to [region/sector]. Here's the proposed pilot framework."
- AR: "نسعى لدعمكم لتقديم هذه المبادرة في [المنطقة/القطاع]. إليكم الإطار التجريبي المقترح."

**Speaker Notes Tone**: Include regulatory citations, policy alignment notes, precedents from other jurisdictions. Flag data privacy and sovereignty considerations. Reference specific government frameworks or initiatives.

## 5. General Overview

**Target**: Internal team, board members, advisory board, general stakeholders, conference audience

**Tone**: Balanced, informative, professional. Cover all dimensions without deep-diving into any single one. Suitable for audiences with mixed backgrounds.

**Opening Hook**: Lead with the vision and the problem it addresses. Set context broadly before narrowing to specifics.

**Content Emphasis**:
- Balanced coverage of problem, solution, market, technology, and business
- High-level architecture without implementation details
- Key metrics and milestones
- Team and resource overview
- Next steps and open questions

**Slide Priority**: All slide types are included at a moderate depth. Nothing is skipped, but nothing goes as deep as audience-specific versions.

**Language Patterns**:
- EN: Neutral professional tone. Mix of business and technical vocabulary at an accessible level.
- AR: لغة مهنية متوازنة. مزيج من المصطلحات التجارية والتقنية بمستوى سهل الفهم.

**Call to Action**: "Questions? Let's discuss next steps."
- AR: "أسئلة؟ دعونا نناقش الخطوات القادمة."

**Speaker Notes Tone**: Comprehensive context for each slide. Include background information that helps explain decisions to people not deeply involved in the project.

## Bilingual Adaptation Notes

All profiles define both Arabic (AR) and English (EN) language patterns. When generating the presentation:

1. **Arabic text must be contextually accurate** — not word-for-word translation. Adapt idioms, business terminology, and cultural framing for Arabic-speaking audiences.
2. **Government/Regulatory Arabic** should use formal Modern Standard Arabic (فصحى) appropriate for official communications.
3. **Customer Demo Arabic** can use slightly more conversational tone while remaining professional.
4. **Technical terms** that have no widely-adopted Arabic equivalent should be kept in English with Arabic transliteration in parentheses where helpful.
5. **Numbers and currency** should appear in both Western (1,2,3) and Arabic-Indic (١,٢,٣) numerals, with the user's locale determining primary display.
