# Budget Allocation Benchmarks

Industry benchmarks for how startups allocate budgets by domain and stage. These inform the Revenue-Based Budget Rule in the budget assessment methodology.

---

## Marketing Budget Benchmarks

| Stage | % of Revenue | Source / Basis |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Pre-revenue / Bootstrapped | 10-15% of projected revenue (scale with actual revenue) | SaaS industry norm for capital-efficient growth |
| Seed stage | 20-35% of revenue | SaaS Capital benchmark: median 30% for <$1M ARR |
| Growth (Series A) | 30-50% of revenue | Bessemer Cloud Index: median 40% S&M for growth SaaS |
| Mature (Series B+) | 20-35% of revenue | Public SaaS companies: median 25-30% S&M |

**Key insight**: Marketing spend as a % of revenue should be highest at Seed/Series A (aggressive growth) and decrease as the company scales and organic channels mature.

**Bootstrap reality**: A bootstrapped founder's "marketing budget" is their time. Cash marketing spend should only begin when revenue covers it. Rule of thumb: don't spend more than 30% of last month's revenue on marketing.

## Infrastructure & Supporting Systems Benchmarks

| Stage | Typical Spend | Allocation Method |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Pre-revenue / Bootstrapped | $0-$500/month | Free tiers + open-source only; manual processes for support |
| Early traction (< $10K MRR) | $500-$2,000/month | Cheapest SaaS that works; upgrade when it breaks |
| Growth ($10K-$50K MRR) | $2,000-$8,000/month | Right-sized SaaS; consider dedicated DevOps |
| Scale ($50K+ MRR) | $8,000-$25,000/month | Enterprise tools where justified; redundancy; monitoring |

**Key insight**: Infrastructure should be bottom-up (sum of what's needed), not top-down (% of revenue). But apply a sanity cap: if infrastructure costs exceed 15-20% of revenue at early stage, look for cheaper alternatives.

**Bootstrap reality**: Most supporting systems have free tiers or open-source alternatives that work fine under 1,000 users. Don't buy enterprise tools for a pre-revenue product.

## Development Cost Benchmarks

| Stage | Typical Allocation | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| Bootstrapped (solo founder) | $0 cash (founder's time) | 60-80% of founder's time on product |
| Pre-seed (1-2 devs) | 50-60% of total budget | Largest expense; hire before spending on marketing |
| Seed (3-8 person team) | 40-50% of total budget | Engineering + product is still the majority |
| Series A (10-20+ team) | 35-45% of total budget | Marketing/sales headcount starts to compete |

**Key insight**: For bootstrapped founders, development "cost" is opportunity cost of time. Don't calculate founder salary as a cost — it creates misleading $0/hr comparisons (this was tried and reverted in a previous iteration).

## Legal & Compliance Benchmarks

| Stage | Typical Spend | What It Covers |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Bootstrapped | $1,000-$5,000/year | Basic incorporation, terms of service template, privacy policy |
| Pre-seed | $5,000-$15,000/year | IP protection, employment agreements, data protection basics |
| Seed | $10,000-$30,000/year | Regulatory compliance, contract review, data protection audit |
| Series A+ | $30,000-$100,000+/year | Ongoing counsel, international compliance, audit preparation |

**Market-specific note (Saudi Arabia)**: PDPL compliance, ZATCA e-invoicing, MOCI registration, and sector-specific regulations (MOMAH for salons, MOH for medical) add compliance costs. Budget SAR 10,000-50,000 ($2,700-$13,300) for legal review of Saudi-specific requirements.

## Total Budget Benchmarks (Annual, All Domains Combined)

| Stage | Typical Annual Burn | Revenue Multiple |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Bootstrapped | $5,000-$30,000 + founder time | 0.5-1.5x of Year 1 revenue (ideally <1x) |
| Pre-seed | $100,000-$400,000 | Often >1x revenue (investing ahead of revenue) |
| Seed | $500,000-$1,500,000 | 0.8-1.5x revenue |
| Series A | $1,500,000-$5,000,000 | 0.7-1.2x revenue |

**Key insight**: Bootstrapped companies MUST spend less than they earn (or very close to it). Funded companies can burn ahead of revenue, but LTV:CAC > 3:1 should still hold.

## Marketplace-Specific Benchmarks

Since many Idea Forge projects are marketplaces (two-sided platforms), these additional benchmarks apply:

| Metric | Benchmark | Source Basis |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Supply-side CAC (provider) | 3-10x customer CAC | Marketplace norm: providers are harder to acquire |
| Demand follows supply | Acquire supply first, then demand | Proven pattern: Uber, Airbnb, Fresha |
| Liquidity threshold | ~30% match rate before scaling demand | Below this, customers churn from empty supply |
| Take rate sustainability | 10-20% for services; 5-15% for goods | Higher take rates require clear value justification |
| Marketing % of GMV | 3-8% of GMV at maturity | Early stage may be higher as % of commission revenue |
